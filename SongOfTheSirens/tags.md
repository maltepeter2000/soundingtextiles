    description: >
      Song of the Sirens explores the tension and fascination between the human—
      rational and technological—world and the animal, mythological, and sometimes
      grotesque realm.

      The work imagines a hypothetical encounter between a human being and a marine
      creature, mediated through a tactile and sonic experience.

      Inspired by the medieval myth of the sirens—half-fish, half-human beings who
      lured sailors with their song—the project takes shape as a digital second skin:
      a glove made of fish leather, embedded with sensors that generate a mysterious
      and seductive sound upon contact with human skin.

      The ambivalence between the sensual quality of the sound and the organic texture
      of this marine skin creates a sense of both attraction and disorientation.

      Song of the Sirens thus becomes a poetic and technological device that reflects
      on desire, transformation, and the fluid boundaries between the human and the
      non-human.
